My name is Michael. Design has been a big part of my life for some time now. It's funny that you start creative design with the lofty ideas of building worlds and universes for others to exist within. There is a touch of megalomania in all designers. Tolkien could have just told a story; simply written down the adventures of Frodo and Sam beyond the Shire, but he went farther in designing his world. He provided a visual reference for his world. He designed communities, languages, and religions to inhabit his world. That vision is the difference between just telling a story and creating an experience.

About Me

So how did I get here? I started with a pipe dream of art and animation at the beginning of my higher education. I've always had a drive for the creative. I soon discovered that web development was a growing technology that provided near immediate results. The problem, however, was that there was no guided means to learn the ins and outs of the fertile land of the internet.

We were taught the psychology of color and form, the nuances of how shape and light make you feel. No one had a clue how to adequately teach what it meant to plan a user interface. Then, they were crude and rudimentary and, by and large, lacked any real aesthetic when it came to interactivity.
